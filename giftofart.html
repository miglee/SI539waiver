Gift of Art

Research and design the digital shift sheet and inventory system to improve patient - volunteer interaction.

Context

Course work with real client: the Gift of Art program of the University Hospital

Role

Researcher and Designer

Process

User Research
Contextual Inquiry
Qualititave analysis
Wireframing

Background

The Gift of Art program has been running for 30 years,  providing art posts for patients in the university hospital.
Our mission is to make suggestion on the  overall operation of the program  and also on designing a new digital management system  to replace the old library card management system.

Research Methods

Background Research

Understand the organization's mission, history, culture, stakeholders and resources;
Dive into research papers about art intervention in healthcare.

Contextual Inquiry

12 Interviews and shadowing with
manager, assistant, volunteers and patients.

Qualitative Analysis

Using affinity wall to find insights and correlations.

Findings

We closely observed the operation of the program, mapped the interview scripts, and found unsatisfied needs.

A need for better
Interacion & communication

The program’s policy of emphasizing on Interaction between volunteers and patients isn't practiced by the volunteers.

We found that volunteers only spend a limited amount of time talking to individual patients even though the manager said "Interaction is more important than number of visit. Spending more time to talk to even just one patient is better than walking through lots of patients. "

Lack of standard promotion channel

There is no standard channels of promotion to all incoming patients 

Flaws in the inventory management

The current inventory management system is unreliable. Human mistakes made it difficult to track the inventory.

Insights / Problem of Focus

We look into the scripts and the operation flow carefully to find insights behind behavior.

Potential flaw in shift sheet design

Right now, a stack of shift sheet represents A SINGLE VOLUNTEER and A SINGLE FLOOR that is assigned to the volunteer.

With this format, volunteers try to finish all the rooms listed on the sheet, and regard this as their duty.  Their responsible attitude prevent them from having meaningful interaction withpatients.

Lack of common communication channel among the program

Policys are not spread through the entire program. 

There's no regular meetings nor common communication board.

Managers communicate with individual volunteers through e-mails. 

Since the shift time is separated, members of the program rarely see each other.

Break points of human mistakes

1. Recording room ID and artwork ID with hand writing often causes mistakes.
2. Need of a "double check" mechanism

Design Suggestions

We not only provided design suggestions but also made a wireframe for the future digital management system. 

Redesign the shift sheets

Quick solution:
redesign the paper shift sheet
1. List the patient rooms continuously without separating them by floors.
2. Put Volunteer’s names and shift information (Date & Time) in the rows rather than on the top of the sheet. 

Long-term solution:
design the new digital shift sheet
1. A page in the shift sheet in the digital system should represent one room instead of one floor and one volunteer.
2. No emphasis on number of rooms visited. 

Stickers

Place stickers beside the beds to promote the service to all incoming patients
-simple, easy and low-cost 

Auto-fill for room numbers
+
artwork preview for double check

No need to write down or select room numbers when exchanging artworks during a shift.

Update: developing an easy tool with google resources

Months after the project, though expressed interest in implementing our design suggestions, the program didn't proceed to develop the digital inventory system.

Being a non-profit organization, the program might not have immediate resources and budget to make the digital system from scratch. I regreted that we didn't succeed in solving the inventory management problem they faced

- so I spent some time on it and made a tool with free google resources (google spreadsheet, google site, google form, google drive), carefully designed to fit their operation flow.
